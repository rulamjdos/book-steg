A Fun Story About Sharing and Friendship

In a jungle where the palm trees sway,
Lived a Stegosaurus named Sammy, so gray.
With plates on his back, all spiky and tall,
He had the best toys, the best of them all!

A shiny red ball, a kite that would soar,
A puzzle of bones from a dino galore.
But Sammy would grumble, he'd huff, and he'd glare,
"My toys are MINE! I'm not gonna share!"

His friends, like Triceratops, gentle and kind,
Would ask, "Can we play?" with hope in their mind.
But Sammy would stomp, his tail in the air,
"No way, no how, I just won't share!"

Pteranodon swooped with a squawk and a frown,
"Sammy, your toys could bring joy to this town!"
But Sammy just roared, "Go find your own stuff!
My toys are for ME, and that's quite enough!"

One day, oh my, his red ball rolled away,
It bounced through the ferns and got lost in the clay.
Sammy was sad, his eyes filled with tears,
"No ball, no fun!" he cried to his peers.

Then Velociraptor, so speedy and small,
Said, "Sammy, don't worry, we'll find your red ball!"
She darted and dashed, with friends by her side,
They searched through the jungle, no place left to hide.

They found it at last, stuck high in a tree,
Triceratops nudged it, and down it came free!
Sammy was thrilled, his heart felt so light,
He saw how his friends made everything right.

"I'm sorry," said Sammy, his voice soft and low,
"I've learned something big, and now I must show.
My toys are more fun when we all play as one,
Let's share and laugh till the day's work is done!"

So Sammy and friends played under the sun,
With kites, balls, and puzzles, they had so much fun.

His plates seemed to gleam with a happier glow,
For sharing, he learned, makes friendship grow!
